About TypeFromPDF

Built to make practice meaningful

We created the only typing practice tool that works with content you already care about — your own PDFs, notes, and code.

Our Mission

Practice with purpose, not with noise

Typing practice has been broken for a long time. Every tool gives you the same random sentences about foxes jumping over dogs or pangrams that have nothing to do with your life. You build typing speed, but not knowledge. You practice, but you don't learn anything you'll actually use.

TypeFromPDF fixes that. Upload any PDF — your study notes, a textbook chapter, a legal document, a research paper — and type over the ghost text. You build real typing speed and genuinely absorb the content at the same time. Two goals, one practice session.

The Story

Why we built this

The idea behind TypeFromPDF came from a simple observation: the best way to remember something is to actively produce it, not just passively read it. Cognitive science has known this for decades — it's called the production effect. Typing out words forces your brain to engage more deeply than simply reading them. Memory retention dramatically improves when you have to reproduce information.

So why are all typing practice tools giving us sentences we don't care about? Why not let people practice with their own material and actually learn while they type?

That question became TypeFromPDF. A tool that respects your time and your intelligence — that helps you build speed with content that actually matters to you, whether that's a chapter of a textbook you're studying, a paper you need to memorize, or code you're trying to internalize.

We also built TypeFromPDF for developers and programmers. Learning a new programming language means reading a lot of syntax. What if you could practice typing real, working code in Python, Go, Rust, TypeScript, and 14 other languages? Not toy examples — actual patterns you'll write on the job.

Technology

How it actually works

  • In-browser PDF parsing — We use PDF.js, a Mozilla-built JavaScript library, to extract text from your PDF completely client-side. Your file is read by your own browser — it never leaves your device.
  • Ghost text overlay — Your PDF's text appears as faded background content. As you type, a transparent overlay tracks your accuracy character by character, highlighting correct characters and marking errors in real time.
  • Live WPM calculation — Words per minute and accuracy are computed continuously as you type. The WPM calculation uses a standard 5-character word definition and updates every 500 milliseconds so you see smooth, accurate numbers.
  • 18 programming languages — Each language includes carefully written, real-world code samples — not toy Hello World programs. The samples demonstrate practical patterns like data structures, async code, type systems, and algorithms.
  • Zero tracking — There are no analytics cookies, no advertising pixels, no tracking scripts. The only data stored locally is your account information and PDF usage count in your own browser's localStorage.
